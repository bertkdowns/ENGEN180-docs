Thermodynamics¶

This continues on from the previous page on temperature. For most stuff in thermodynamics just do the math so that the units cancel correctly, and remember that W = J/s and kW = kJ/s.

Energy vs Power¶

Energy is represented by a capital \(Q\) and measured in Joules or kJ.

Power is represented by a lowercase \(q\). It is rate of energy usage, measured in joules/s or watts.

Mass flow rates¶

Mass flow rates have the symbol \(\dot{m}\). They are measured in g/s or something similar. When you have an equation for energy, if you use a mass flow rate instead of a mass, it gives you power (W) instead of energy (J). You can easily see this with unit cancellation stuff.

Enthalpy¶

Enthalpy is the amount of heat energy something has per gram of it. It is different to temperature - different types of material have different heat capacities, which means more enthalpy per degree of temperature.

Enthalply is represented as \(h\), in units kJ/g or kJ/kg.

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}Q = m h\\q = \dot{m} h\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

Thermal Efficiency¶

Thermal efficiency is how much usable heat energy is generated. Calculated by:

\[\frac{Usable \ Energy}{Total\ Heat\ Energy}\]

Energy required & Gravity¶

\[Q = m g \Delta z\]
Where:

  • Q is energy required

  • g is accelleration due to gravity - 9.81 m/s

  • \(\Delta\) z is the change in height

Sensible & Latent heat¶

Sensible heat is heat energy absorbed by something becoming hotter. remember \(Q = m c \Delta T\) for sensible heat, where

  • c is the heat capacity of the thing - \(J/g.^{\circ}K\)

  • \(\Delta\) T is the change in temperature

  • m is mass

Latent heat is heat energy absorbed by something changing state - it takes energy to change from a liquid to a gas. When energy is being absorbed this way you don’t see a change in temperature, you instead see a change in state. The amount of heat absorbed by the change in state is represented as \(\lambda\) in units J/g
